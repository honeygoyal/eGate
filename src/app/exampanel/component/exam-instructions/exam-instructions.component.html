Instructions

These instructions are based on GATE 2021 pattern.

Please read the instructions carefully

General Instructions

  1. 1. Total duration of examination is {{ exam.duration }} minutes.
  2. 2. The clock will be set at the server. The countdown timer in the top right corner of screen will display the remaining time available for you to complete the examination. When the timer reaches zero, the examination will end by itself. You will not be required to end or submit your examination.
  3. 3. The Question Palette displayed on the right side of screen will show the status of each question using one of the following symbols:
    1 You have not visited the question yet.
    2 You have not answered the question.
    3 You have answered the question.
    4 You have NOT answered the question, but have marked the question for review.
    5 The question(s) "Answered and Marked for Review" will be considered for evaluation.
  4. 4. The Marked for Review status for a question simply indicates that you would like to look at that question again.

Navigating to a Question:

  1. 5. To answer a question, do the following:
    1. a. Click on the question number in the Question Palette at the right of your screen to go to that numbered question directly. Note that using this option does NOT save your answer to the current question.
    2. b. Click on Save & Next to save your answer for the current question and then go to the next question.
    3. c. Click on Mark for Review & Next to save your answer for the current question, mark it for review, and then go to the next question.

Answering a Question :

  1. 6. Procedure for answering a multiple choice type question:
    1. a. To select your answer, click on the button of one of the options
    2. b. To deselect your chosen answer, click on the button of the chosen option again or click on the Clear Response button
    3. c. To change your chosen answer, click on the button of another option
    4. d. To save your answer, you MUST click on the Save & Next button
    5. e. To mark the question for review, click on the Mark for Review & Next button.
    1. 7. To change your answer to a question that has already been answered, first select that question for answering and then follow the procedure for answering that type of question.

Navigating through sections:

  1. 8. Sections in this question paper are displayed on the top bar of the screen. Questions in a section can be viewed by clicking on the section name. The section you are currently viewing is highlighted.
  2. 9. After clicking the Save & Next button on the last question for a section, you will automatically be taken to the first question of the next section.
  3. 10. You can shuffle between sections and questions anytime during the examination as per your convenience only during the time stipulated.
  4. 11. Candidate can view the corresponding section summary as part of the legend that appears in every section above the question palette.

Paper Specific Instructions

Read the following instructions carefully.

  1. 1 .Go through various question palette symbols and understand their meanings before starting the examination.
  2. 2. After the start of the examination, you can view all the questions by clicking on the Question Paper button on the screen.
  3. 3. The question paper consists of total {{ exam.totalQuestion }} questions and total {{ exam.totalMarks }} marks.
  4. 4. Use the data given in the question while answering that question. If such data are not given, and the paper has useful data icon, then the same can be viewed by clicking on the Useful Data button that appears at the top, right-hand side of the screen.
  5. 5. The question paper consists of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ), Multiple Select Questions (MSQ) and Numerical Answer Type (NAT).
    a) Multiple choice questions (MCQ) have four choices A, B, C, D, out of which only ONE is the correct answer. Select the correct answer by clicking on the radio button (O) placed before the choice, with the help of the mouse.
    b) For numerical answer type (NAT) questions, a numerical answer should be entered with the help of the mouse and the virtual numeric keypad, which will appear below the question.
    c) Multiple Select questions (MSQ) have four choices A, B, C, D, out of which  multiple choices are the correct answer. Select the correct answer by clicking on the radio button (O) placed before the choice, with the help of the mouse.
  6. 6. All those questions that are not attempted will carry zero marks. However, wrong answers for multiple choice questions (MCQ) will carry NEGATIVE marks. For multiple choice type questions, a wrong answer will lead to deduction of 1/3 marks for a 1-mark question and 2/3 marks for a 2-mark question. There is no negative marking for NAT and MSQ questions.
  7. 7. Your answers will be saved on the server periodically and also at the end of the examination. The examination will stop automatically at the end of {{ exam.duration }} minutes.
  8. 8. You are not allowed to leave the examination hall until the completion of the examination.
  9. 9. Charts, graph sheets, calculators and mathematical tables are NOT allowed in the examination hall. You must use the scribble pad provided to you in the examination hall for your rough work, which must be returned at the end of the examination.
